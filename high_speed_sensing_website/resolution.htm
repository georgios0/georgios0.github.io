Distinguishing spatial details in high contrast images

 

Given a black and white image, such as a text document, it would be important to know the smallest feature in the image we can distinguish using the sensor. This depends on a number of factors such as the ratio of the feature to the total image and the integration time. A series of tests have been devised to answer this question.

 

In the tests that follow, we are forming a 2‐D image which varies only in the horizontal direction. This is because we are assuming that the image formed by the sensor is the image of a line. To smooth out the results, we are averaging the values along the columns.

Overall Conclusion on above tests

Maximum and zero intensity (e.g. black and white) images can be distinguished with a very high spatial detail. For example, a white object which covers 1/200th of the total area can be easily distinguished from the black background. In addition, reducing the integration time to a small number, does not affect the ability to distinguish black and white images. Hence, when the contrast is large, we can be confident enough that we can infer very high spatial detail.
